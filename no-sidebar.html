My Ethos

I believe each of us of us is here with a purpose, and by living that purpose not only will we each experience greater happiness and fulfilment, but we'll also be making this earth a better place to live. Knowing that there is a meaning to one's existence, and a purpose for what one does are the essential ingredients of motivation, and motivation is the fuel that drives success.

When we ask ourselves "what is the meaning of my life?" Or, "what is my purpose here?" The answer will not be found in searching for what we can get for ourselves, but rather in how we can serve others. Therefore, the question to ask when building a career doing what you love is "how can I use my skills and passions to serve others?"

If we are feeling stuck in life, it's only because our thinking is stuck: our thoughts create our experience. In them lies the creative power to shift our experience to anything that we put our focus on. What is that you want to show up in your life? What is it that you a strong, impossible to ignore, urge to do or be? How does your desire fit in to the bigger picture and help others? Focus on that goal and go after it.

My backgound

My interest in self-development started in my late teens when I could no longer tolerate the incessant cycling of negative thoughts. I was convinced that everyone was judging me critically and had an often fraught relationship with family members. At that point I picked up a book by Louise Hay that was on our shelves., and read her words on affirmations. She explained that it is possible to heal one's life by healing one's thoughts. I decided to give it a go and wrote down a list of affirmations that I would conciously repeat to myself as a way of breaking the existing pattern of thoughts. After a few weeks I noticed things were improving in my relationships and how I felt because I had taken off the "dark glasses" of negativity.

This experience set me on a journey of exploration to learn more about how our thought, beliefs and philosophical outlook can be fine tuned to bring about the highest and best experience of life that each of us are capable of.

I believe that developing mastery over our minds is the path to realising our potential, making a positive impact on the world and creating the lives we desire.

My story

Music has been close to my heart from as early as I can remember and this led me to doing a music degree. A bad experience in first year knocked my confidence and so I dropped performance in favour of academic modules that I enjoyed. However, after completing an MA in Ethnomusicology, I realised I missed making music with others. I decided to continue violin and piano studies and accepted an invitation to join a community Baroque Orchestra.
